What are stocks?

Stocks are parts of large multimillion, billion and sometime trillion dollar companies. When you buy a share of a stock you are buying a small part of a corporation, and as the company grows the share value increases and you can sell for a profit. You can buy and sell shares through a broker such as Charle Schwab, TD Ameritrade, or Robinhood(lots of drama).

Risk and Volatility

The market can be extremely risky and volatile at times. Market corrections and crashes are healthy and part of the long term success of the stock market. Although, the broad market generally goes up in the long term, the same cannot be said for individual stocks which makes them much riskier. Volatility is how much a security is moving either up or down. There are times when the overall market is very volatile and times where it is stable and only a select few stocks are considered volatle.

How To Learn More

With the internet you can learn almost anything you want. There are YouTubers who teach people about the market, there are people who sell courses online. You can also learn on your own, there are many books on investing and stocks as well as many online resources. Some books include, The Intelligent Investor or Security Analysis both by Benjamin Graham, there are countless others even for beginners who know nothing.
Online resources include Investopedia, many articles online are generally surface level or news oriented but can go in depth at certain times. Finding a mentor or people more experienced than you can also be very helpful.
